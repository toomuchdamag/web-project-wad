Foundation Course

International Year Zero (IYZ)

Key Details

Award: Foundation
Duration: One year full-time
Location: DMU Dubai campus

Course overview

The International Year Zero (IYZ) is an essential gateway program that offers specialized pathways into various undergraduate degree programs at DMU Dubai, including business, law, social sciences, engineering, computing, and art and design.

The curriculum for each pathway is designed to equip students with the foundational knowledge and skills needed for their chosen fields. It focuses on enhancing English language proficiency, developing effective study habits, and providing in-depth subject knowledge. Additionally, the program familiarizes students with DMU’s teaching methodologies, feedback mechanisms, and assessment practices, thereby preparing them to meet the challenges of their degree programs and achieve future success.

Upon enrollment in the IYZ, students will declare their intended degree; however, they have the flexibility to switch to another program within the same discipline if necessary.

To ensure smooth progression into their chosen degrees, students will undertake English for Academic Purposes (EAP) and are required to attain a minimum IELTS score of 6.0 (or 6.5 for Psychology). This thorough preparation is crucial for their academic journey ahead.

Key Features

The curriculum is designed to provide newcomers to a discipline with a solid foundation, enabling them to develop essential skills and create a portfolio that supports their successful transition into their chosen undergraduate program.

For those who already possess some experience in their selected field, the curriculum will strengthen and expand their existing knowledge and capabilities.

These programs feature innovative teaching and learning strategies, offering specialized pathways across various subject areas.

The English for Academic Purposes (EAP) curriculum emphasizes the development of language skills and discipline-specific expertise. Its unique design aligns with the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), making De Montfort University the only higher education institution in the UK designated as a global hub for one of the SDGs—specifically SDG 16, which aims to promote peace, justice, and strong institutions.

Teaching staff in specialized areas are also involved in the degree programs, ensuring that IYZ students have a strong connection to their future departments and the campus. Program leaders, who are highly experienced professionals, are dedicated to delivering a quality education and are available to address any questions or concerns regarding the accreditation process or course content.

The program's diverse student body creates valuable opportunities for networking and gaining broader cultural insights throughout the IYZ year, which will benefit graduates in their future degrees and career paths. Additionally, this program includes a four-year option that integrates the International Year Zero for applicants with non-standard entry qualifications.

Course Structure

Each specialist pathway has its own term 2 programme of study that finally prepares students for their chosen degree.

Being proficient in English is crucial for effectively communicating with your tutors and understanding lectures. Consequently, you will engage in English studies throughout both terms, with modules designed to complement your academic study skills and specialized pathway modules, thereby enhancing your learning and facilitating your progress towards your degree.

The English for Academic Purposes (EAP) module, conducted during terms 1 and 2, will focus on improving your skills in reading, writing, listening, and speaking. This curriculum will be seamlessly integrated into a content-based syllabus that uniquely incorporates the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) as a framework for teaching relevant topics.

This module in term 1 assists students in transitioning from their previous educational backgrounds to the experience of studying in British Higher Education, specifically at De Montfort University. It offers students the chance to practice and enhance various academic study skills crucial for successful university learning, while also fostering self-awareness and providing insights into the utilization and effectiveness of learning platforms and academic discussion forums.

In term 1, all students in Business & Law, Engineering, Computing, and Social Sciences will participate in a tailored curriculum focused on mathematics and statistics, which will reinforce their existing knowledge and enhance their learning for future modules and degree requirements. In contrast, Art and Design students have distinct needs, so they will enroll in a separate module that introduces them to various technical and skill-based processes essential for art and design. This curriculum serves as a vital component of a shared creative practice 'toolkit' for Level 4 study and beyond.

In the 'Creative Portfolio' module, students can enhance their specialized knowledge and focus within the Art & Design Pathway. Project 1, titled Creative Brief, will help students understand how to respond to and create creative briefs that are relevant to their degree specializations.

Project 2, known as the Personal Project, consists of two interconnected components: a short essay and a personal art or design project portfolio aligned with the student’s degree focus. Research for the essay will commence concurrently with Project 1 to provide context and inform their negotiated project brief for Project 2.

The 'Context of Business' module provides students with a comprehensive understanding of the operations and functioning of companies. This overview includes insights into the legal frameworks and accounting obligations that businesses must adhere to. We will use real-world examples of challenges faced by executives in today’s business environment to highlight the module’s main concepts.

Throughout the course, we strive to cultivate a critical perspective on various issues, utilizing case studies and specialized academic literature as our framework.

In the 'Problem Solving and Programming' module, students will engage with practical examples of computing applications, allowing them to grasp the key concepts and significance of algorithms, even if they have minimal or no prior programming experience.

Students will learn about data representation and develop problem-solving and logical skills while also gaining hands-on experience in creating and running simple programs. This preparation will equip them for the programming elements of their undergraduate studies, using transferable skills that are essential to the principles of programming.

The ‘Foundation Engineering Applications and Mathematics’ module equips students with essential concepts, knowledge, and skills in mathematics and engineering theory and practice required for undergraduate engineering studies. Students will gain a thorough understanding of indices, logarithmic and exponential functions and their respective graphs, as well as the differences between polar coordinates and the Cartesian coordinate system. They will also explore matrices, including operations such as scalar multiplication, addition, and subtraction.

The curriculum will advance to cover determinants, matrix multiplication, inverse matrices, and solving simultaneous equations, followed by a deeper examination of sequences and series, differentiation, and integration.

The 'Foundations in Psychology' course introduces students to the key theoretical approaches and perspectives within the field of psychology. Students will learn to discuss and critically evaluate these theories by relating them to everyday situations and examining their practical applications in the real world. The curriculum also investigates how theory and research shape various viewpoints in psychology. Moreover, students will cover fundamental research methods and important topics such as ethics. They will gain familiarity with five core areas: biological, cognitive, developmental, personality, and social psychology. Additionally, students will explore how research supports psychological claims while keeping ethical principles in mind.
